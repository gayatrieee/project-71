ACCREDITATION

In October 2017, International School Manila hosted a Visiting Team comprising representatives from the Council of International Schools (CIS) and the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC). Team Members were provided with a 400-page Self-evaluation Study, completed by ISM faculty and staff and with input from students, parents and Trustees. The Team spent a week on campus and, at the end of their visit, submitted a comprehensive report commending the school for its achievements and recommending possible avenues for analysis and development. It is evident that there are excellent relationships between students and teachers characterized by mutual respect and an understanding that they share the community that is ISM.

100 YEARS OF ISM, 100 YEARS OF UNITY IN DIVERSITY!

School Year 2019-2020 marked our 100th year! IS Manila, formerly known as the American School first opened its doors in 1920 with eight teachers and fifty students. Today, we have grown to over 2,300 students and 400 multicultural faculty and staff with more than 100 nationalities represented. It is an extremely exciting year to be at IS Manila and celebrate our Centennial Anniversary. Since 1920, our Green and Gold colors have carried us through a vibrant history and united our community in solidarity.

GIVING TO ISM

ISM is a non-profit school and receives no financial support from governments or religious institutions. Tuition income, charitable annual gifts and other fundraising make up the yearly operating budget. Annual Giving donations support all aspects of the operating budget, including the cost of attracting the best teachers, offering excellent academic and co-curricular programs, and equipping and maintaining a state-of-the-art infrastructure. In short, these donations provide the excellence in programming that is the hallmark of an ISM education. Gifts of all amounts are greatly appreciated and demonstrate the community support of the School. Each year we receive donations ranging from $1 to over $20,000. Gifts made to the International School in Manila Foundation, a US 501(c)(3) organization, are tax-deductible for US taxpayers to the full extent of US tax code (please consult with your tax advisor).
